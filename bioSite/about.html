About Hannah Ashburn

Hannah is an inspiring artist. She is passionate about her artwork and people around her. Hannah fights for peoples right while at the same time making great artwork. She was a Seventh-day Adventist growing up, so she attended private schools that unfortunately did not have art classes due to the funding. Hannah had to learn artwork on her own with the limited tools given to her. Her supplies came from her parents and gifts from friends. When she graduated high school, she did not know exactly what she wanted to do. When she first started college, she only knew that she wanted to do something with art. Hannah first attended at Union College, in Lincoln, Nebraska as an Art Education major.

Hannah is also very spontaneous. Hannah went to Italy the start of her sophomore year with only $2000 to live on throughout the whole year. Once Hannah came back from Italy her junior year, she then decided to change her major to Studio Art to acquire more art skills. At this point her true accomplishments begins.

Hannah graduated with her bachelors while being a Social Vice President on the ASB organization. This organization is known as the Associated Student Body that creates events for the whole school.

Hannah is now studying at the University of Nebraska Lincoln to achieve her master’s in Art History. While studying at UNL she also is working at the Great Plains Art Museum. She is extremely talented which got her the opportunity to curate her own show using the museum’s permanent art holdings and collection. This was huge for her because she was able to show how people express women being seen within a painting and how people perceive them. Hannah is devoted to human rights as she also donated art pieces to the Black Lives Matter Lincoln Chapter action. These art pieces acquired a significant price, so it was a huge personal accomplishment because it for an organization she was inspired by and passionate about. If you ever want to see Hannah’s artwork you can see it inside the Noyes Gallery. She is also doing her fourth Art and Garden show this year at the Sunken Gardens at the beginning of September.
